A1.1 — Possessivos · Conectores · Refeições

A Regra Principal
The possessive agrees with the thing owned — not the owner. And the article (o/a/os/as) always travels with it.

o meu carro — my car (carro is masc → o + meu)
a minha garrafa — my bottle (garrafa is fem → a + minha)

teu/tua/teus/tuas — informal equivalent of seu/sua. Used with tu. In Lisbon you'll hear both, but your instructor uses você → seu/sua as the default. Note: seu/sua can also mean "his/her/their" depending on context.

Refeições — Meal Times
The meal verbs and time expressions from class. de manhã ≠ amanhã (tomorrow) — different words, easy to confuse.
Meia de leite — the classic Portuguese milky coffee. More milk than an espresso, less than a latte. Order with um café extra for stronger. Pronounced /meia de leite/ — leite sounds like /lait/. At a café: Queria uma meia de leite, por favor.
