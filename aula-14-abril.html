Regra de Posição
Onde vai o advérbio de frequência?
Positivo → depois do verbo
sempre muitas vezes frequentemente
sujeito + verbo + frequência
Eu cozinho sempre à noite.
Negativo → antes do verbo
raramente nunca
sujeito + frequência + verbo
Eu nunca cozinho à tarde.
nunca and raramente go before the verb because they are negatives — they change the nature of the action. Sempre, muitas vezes, frequentemente go after the verb because they simply describe how often a positive action occurs.

Expressões de Opinião
Acho que…
"I think that…" — more colloquial, more common in everyday speech
From the verb achar (to find / to think). This is the natural, everyday way to express an opinion in Lisbon. Use this one by default.
Acho que é caro.
I think it's expensive.
Acho que é barato.
I think it's cheap.
Acho que o restaurante é bom.
I think the restaurant is good.
Penso que…
"I think that…" — synonymous with acho que, slightly more formal
From pensar (to think). Means exactly the same as acho que. Slightly more formal register but both are common. Your instructor confirmed they are effectively interchangeable at this level.
Penso que é caro.
I think it's expensive.
Penso que Lisboa é uma cidade fantástica.
I think Lisbon is a fantastic city.
Não sei se…
"I don't know if…"
Used to express uncertainty. Note: se (if) — not que (that). The s and the following word blend in speech.
Não sei se é caro ou barato.
I don't know if it's expensive or cheap.
Não sei se o restaurante está aberto.
I don't know if the restaurant is open.
Pronunciation note: Acho que é — the e in que blends into the é that follows. You won't hear a gap. Listen: the phrase sounds like "Acho qu'é". Same in Não sei se é — the e and é run together.
ei → /ai/
In European Portuguese, the letter combination ei is typically pronounced /ai/ — like the English word "eye." This is one of the features that most distinguishes pt-PT from Brazilian Portuguese, where ei sounds more like "ay."

Nota — chamar-se
chamar-se = to be called / to call oneself. A reflexive verb — the -se is a reflexive pronoun that attaches to the verb. In the first person: Eu chamo-me Dave. You encountered this in class from an anecdote — the reflexive pattern will be studied more fully later.
