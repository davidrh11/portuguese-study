Nota — SER vs. ESTAR

Both mean "to be" but are not interchangeable. Ser is for permanent identity: Sou de Miami (I'm from Miami). Estar is for temporary states and location: Estou em Lisboa (I'm in Lisbon — right now) / Estou bem (I'm fine — today). This distinction is the single most common error English speakers make. When in doubt, ask yourself: is this permanent fact or current state?
